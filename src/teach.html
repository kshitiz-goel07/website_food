Teach Your Kids To Make Their Own Lunch



HERE'S HOW IT'S DONE:



1. ESTABLISH GUIDELINES
Post a chart on the fridge with lunch fixings sorted by category. (See above for a sample chart to get you started.) Let your kids pack whatever combination appeals to them, as long as it includes one choice from each category.

2. INVOLVE YOUR KIDS
Talk to your kids about the new lunch routine and get their input into its implementation. Ask them if there are certain foods they want you to buy for the week, show them where you keep thelunch box.Teach them very basic knife skills, so they feel comfortable cutting up a bell pepper or slicing a pear.



3. FIGURE OUT THE SCHEDULE
Make sure you know how this will work logistically: do your kids want to put together a meal plan for the week ahead of time or just wing it? Do they want to pack all their lunches on Sunday or put them together each night? (I highly recommend not waiting until morning! Dithering over what to pack could start your day on a sour note.) We'll talk more about logistics in next week's installment, but for now, start thinking about how this could be practically implemented in your house.

4.SET THE STAGE FOR SUCCESS
Each week, prepare or buy one item that can be the foundation of multiple meals, like grilled chicken or pasta. Challenge your kids to use this healthy base to create meals with flavors that appeal to them. This will give kids who are unused to cooking a helpful starting point and will get them thinking about food in a new way.
